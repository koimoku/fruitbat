My husband had a special type of bone cancer that needed to be removed in an operation. The anesthesiologist administered him with anesthesia during the surgery, and after the operation was complete, I was allowed to visit him. He was still clearly under the effects of the anesthesia, and mumbled words I could never forget. Words of affirmation flooded out of his drunkenly mouth, such as: “Is that my amazing, beautiful, wife?” and “I love you so much”. Even when he was unconscious, he had the nerve to tell me he could not imagine a world marrying someone other than me. I was flabbergasted, and this ordeal made me realize how much I care for him.

I want to be with him forever.
